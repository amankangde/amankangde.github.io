Mechanic: Zero-Gravity Floating / Underwater Floating 🌌🌊

This unique game mechanic introduces a physics-based floating system, where players navigate environments that simulate zero-gravity or underwater movement. The mechanic is designed to challenge players’ spatial awareness and precision by altering traditional movement controls, creating a distinct and immersive gameplay experience.

Key Features of the Mechanic:

🔹Omnidirectional Movement:

Players can move freely in all directions—up, down, left, right, and diagonally—mimicking the sensation of being in a zero-gravity or underwater environment.

🔹Inertia and Momentum:

Movement carries inertia, requiring players to plan their actions carefully. Once a direction is chosen, the player continues to drift slightly before coming to a stop, adding a layer of strategy to navigation.

🔹Environmental Interaction:

Objects and obstacles in the environment also float or drift, reacting dynamically to player contact, creating a living, reactive world.

🔹Buoyancy Simulation:

If the mechanic is underwater-based, certain objects may float to the surface, while others sink, allowing players to manipulate these objects to solve puzzles or access new areas.

🔹Precision Challenges:
Floating through tight spaces or avoiding obstacles requires careful control, testing the player’s timing and coordination.

🔹Evolving Gameplay:

The floating mechanic is tied to specific environmental challenges, such as navigating currents, avoiding debris, or using the environment’s zero-gravity properties to bypass hazards or reach hidden areas.

🔹Adaptive Player Abilities:

Players can acquire upgrades or items that enhance control in floating environments, such as boosters for speed bursts or grappling tools for anchoring to objects.
